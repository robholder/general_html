BlinkScript - Bounds

Question

In Nuke's BlinkScript, why do the bounds parameters of a src image have '2' after the axis?

  • src.bounds.x2
  • src.bounds.y2

In BlinkScript (used within Nuke), the bounds.x2 and bounds.y2 parameters represent the maximum (or right/bottom) boundary of the source image in the respective axes.

Here's the breakdown:

  • bounds.x1 and bounds.y1 define the minimum coordinates (left/top boundary).
  • bounds.x2 and bounds.y2 define the maximum coordinates (right/bottom boundary).

This effectively specifies the rectangular bounding box of the image.

The reason for the 2 suffix is to distinguish it from x1/y1, ensuring a clear reference to the full extent of the image.

This is particularly useful when working with shaders or processing pixels, allowing precise control over what region of the image is being manipulated.
