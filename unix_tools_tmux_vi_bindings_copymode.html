Tmux - Vi Bindings (Copy Mode)

Normally, you use your mouse or arrow keys to scroll through terminal history; this setting lets you use the H, J, K, L keys and other Vim shortcuts instead.

How to Invoke (Vi Copy Mode)

To enter the mode where these commands work, press your Tmux prefix (usually 'Ctrl+b') followed by '[' (the left square bracket):

  • <prefix> [ - Enter copy mode
  • You will see a line counter appear in the top-right corner, indicating you are in Copy Mode.

Common Commands

Once inside Copy Mode, you can use these standard Vi motions:

  • Navigation: k (up), j (down), h (left), l (right).
  • Jumping: w (next word), b (back a word), 0 (start of line), $ (end of line).
  • Searching: / followed by your search term (press n for next match).
  • Scrolling: <Ctrl> u (page up), <Ctrl> d (page down).

How to Select and Copy Text

  • Move the cursor to where you want to start.
  • Press <Space> (or <Shift> v) to begin the selection (visual mode).
  • Move the cursor to highlight the text.
  • Press <Enter> to copy the text into the Tmux buffer and exit the mode.
  • To Paste, press your <prefix> ] - (Ctrl+b) followed by ].
