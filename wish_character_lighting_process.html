Flat & Stylized

  • Ambient
  • Region Blurs
  • (munging/bilateral)

Try to keep each element (character) as one unified tone (or just a few). Minimal lights (number of lights)

Building Up The Lighting

Lights

  • Ambient:
    • Virtually SurfaceColor
    • Base of lighting
    • This has a tint for the shot
    • Expose up more than we're used to
  • Key:
    • Maybe adjust values on darks (hair?):
      • Especially when foreground hair blends with foreground hair
  • Top light:
    • to give some shape
  • Bounce Light:
    • Really soft
  • Rim Light:
    • If needed

The 'ambient' light has a fake (mapped) shadowing on the faces.

Munging

  • Remove Rim from image before Munging section
  • Goal is to group the lighting

Region Blur Nodes

  • 'Preview' shows the mattes:
    • Red:
      • Shadows
    • Green:
      • Midtones
    • Blue:
      • Highlights
  • Blur size for regions:
    • Brights have more blur than darks
    • Adjust dark, mid, lights for good spread
  • Region blur for:
    • Hair
    • Character
  • Terminator Softness:
    • lower - sharper
    • higher - softer

Note: use scatter in DLight to soften the terminator also

  • InnerBlur:
    • Flatten Eyes
    • for eye softening only
  • wish_bilateral:
    • Gives brushy breakup
    • Adds texture map influence also
    • Mix: 0.5 as a starting point
    • Keep it subtle (don't remove too much detail)
    • Keep it mostly off the skin

Edge pooling

This takes the tagged lighting groups and uses a luninance key to create a mask.

Fake Rim

Edge effect, with glow effect options. Try blending with the 'real' rim.

Can look procedural or too 'graphic'.

Note: Valentino doesn't rim well

SSAO (Screen Space Ambient Occlusion)

Adds some shaping back to the character.

  • Two WISH_SSAO nodes:
    • Small detail
    • Large detail

Larger WISH_SSAO node values slows comp

  • Key lunimance (tag) is used in this area.

Edge Breakup

  • Breaks up the GC look
  • There's a face matte to protect the face
