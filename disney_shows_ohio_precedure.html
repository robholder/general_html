Color Keys

  • The process starts with Painted Keys/Color Keys:
    • Leaves room for interpretation

Foundation Lighting

Often materials are not present.

  • General light directions
    • Character focus is not important
  • Mood
  • One click render
  • No layers, overall render
  • Foundation rig is published for upstream departments.

Foundation Plus

  • This is an unofficial chaotic race towards the keys and final

Key Lighting

  • Covers camera directions as groups.
  • Process 'unlocks' shots for lighters.

Shot Packets

  • Light groups - similar or 'same as' shots.
  • Key shot of a packet will feed the secondary shots

One-off Shots

  • Uniquely lit shots
  • Typically, they don't match other shots

Technical Challenges

  • Stuff to figure out things in the scene
  • Reflections, shadowing, shot-in-shot etc.

Render Levels

Shots are rendered at lower quality:

  • Lower samples (32, 64)
  • Steps (2s, 5s, 10s or FML; as directed)
  • Creative approvals usually OK at this level
  • Mono

Techcheck level is full resolution, samples and frames.

  • 128 and up?
  • Mono & Stereo

Techcheck

  • Check Shotgrid:
    • All upstream items need to have an empty circle icon
    • Check with Production Managers before render
  • Clean up noise
  • Clean up comp issues:
    • matte lines
    • rotos
    • clamped values
  • Render these:
    • Mono
    • Stereo
  • Check stereo friendly results
  • Show Supervisor before submission to DPix Techcheck:
    • Do not change after Supervisor review before submission
  • Do not change AFTER submission

(Techcheck looks exposure up 3 stops and down 3 stops ready for HDR.)

Stereo Check for Lighting

  • Not a visual check, more technical requirements.
  • Label 3d elements:
    • wraps
    • rotos
  • OK:
    • Rotos
    • 3d compatible nodes
    • Wraps
  • Not OK
    • Rotos 'in-line'
    • Lens flares (these need approved workflow)
