Deep-Dives - Lines

Inspect Shuffles

  • Take a look at the shuffles (into the lines section of Nuke):
    • Volume Luminance
    • Luminance
    • Specular Luminance

These should have sensible data in them for lines to work.

Linart Node

  • Utility Input: Currently using utility 4k node.
    • Depth
    • Normal
    • Custom Mattes:
      • Look created maps (or Geo ID) Input
      • Based generally on boundaries of geo
    • Other Lines (Tab):
      • Choose Opacity map first:
        • PosSphere Based Lines
          • Alternative to Normals lines
        • Use ID lines:
          • Clean simple lines on geo
          • Fills holes for the most part.


  • There is a Character Distance Adjust section in the node:
    • Reduce as characters recede into space (less lines)
    • Usually this is dialed down a lot
  • There is a Line Opacity override:
    • Default is 0.5 (a median setting)
    • usually a heavy hammer

Line Breakup

BrokenEdgeSimple

  • Enable Small/Medium/Large:
    • breaks up line
  • Enable Erode:
    • changes width

Dilate Round

  • Use as a last resort (after BrokenEdgesSimple)
  • Tool to reduce weight
  • Uses mattes too

Watercolor Paper Grades

  • Uses maps to add 'lost and found' to the lines
  • Adds color (I think?)

Layers of Interest

  • Adjustments to reduce the lines on background areas for the most part.
  • Not too many changes here - Look will be providing some too.

Specular

  • Mix some specular bright spots onto lines (to fade the lines)
  • Lighten line color around bright areas
  • This is mostly used for faces

Volume Luminance

  • Controls to reduce/remove from volume areas

CC Lines

  • Adjustment to finished line output to taste

Tips

  • Try to achieve 'lost and found' rather then just 'watercolor paper' look
  • Look at Alpha to see what the lines actually are.
  • Normals are generally too busy:
    • good for faces however.
  • Depth Dilate:
    • These nodes Bloats the maps
