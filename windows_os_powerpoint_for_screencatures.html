Creating an Animated GIF of a Screencapture on Windows

If you want to create an animated screencapture, you can use Powerpoint on Windows OS (and record a remote session's window via the windows host).

Open Powerpoint

In Windows Start Menu:

  • Open Powerpoint:
    • Double click on 'Blank presentation'
    • Delete the 'Click to add title' and 'Click to add subtitle':
      • Drag across the two items to select
      • Hit the delete key

Capture the screen as a video:

  • Click on either:
    • Insert menu
    • Record menu
  • Click on 'Screen Recording'
  • Select area of screen (smaller being better of course)
  • Controls should appear at the top of the active monitor
    • Switch audio on or off
    • Switch cursor on or off
  • Click 'Record'
    • (The controls will disappear)

Completion of Recording

  • At completion of capture
    • Move cursor to the top of the screen to re-activate the record controls.
      • Click on the Stop icon
      • or
      • 'Windows Key + Shift + Q'

The capture will be placed onto your Powerpoint slide.

Create an MP4

  • To save as an MP4, just right click on your media/video item and choose:
    • Save media as..
  • The MP4 video file is ready.

GIF

To create a GIF, you have to export the whole 'slide'.

It's likely your slide is bigger than the capture (or smaller), so to prepare the slide format:

  • If you click on the capture video element, you'll see its properties under the Video Format menu tool bar.
  • The size will be in inches. Make a note of the size
  • Go to the Design menu and click on:
    • Slide Size > Custom Slide Size...
    • Enter the size of the catured video.
    • If correct the video should sit snugly on the slide.

To export the GIF:

  • Click on File menu (don't panic that your stuff has disappeared, it's still there if you click the left arrow in a circle.
  • Select 'Export'
  • Click on Create an Animated GIF
    • Choose a size/quality (I liked 720 at 15fps in terms of compression/size)
    • Click on 'Create GIF'
  • Save somewhere that you'll remember!!

It'll take a while too 'cook': if you check it too soon, you'll get a Windows warning (something like, 'open in another window' or something).

  • Just wait!
  • Eventually, you'll be able to open and see it in its full glory.

Post your video somewhere and glow with pride.
